Activating UK Politicians in the EU Referendum
Leah Bae·Tuesday, 17 May 2016
Hi there — thanks for reading. I’m Leah Bae and this is my application to the EMEA Politics and Government Outreach Manager role based in London. Akin to Facebook’s mantra, I’d rather show you my credentials than talk about them. Below, I’ve compiled a proposal to get one of the UK’s leading politicians on board with Facebook.
The EU referendum is coming up on June 23rd. This election is the most important poll in a generation, and the vote will be a close one with serious consequences attached. As part of this historic vote, leaders from both Leave and Remain sides will be campaigning to shape the debate. This election is not the digital election; both campaigns already understand well the profound impacts of social media and use it widely, with Vote Leave and StrongerIn concentrating on Facebook as their platform of choice. This election will be the conversational election, and the effectiveness of online debate will be informed by the quality of creative and key players that get involved.
It’s important that the key players are able to influence the conversation. One of the biggest influencers in this referendum is Home Secretary Theresa May.
These are the top reasons that the Home Secretary is the ideal candidate to activate in the EU referendum right now:
  1. The major opportunity with the Home Secretary is that she has yet to develop herself a presence on any social media platform -- not even Twitter.
  2. The Home Secretary has been facing huge backlash over her recent decisions, such as her calls to withdraw from the European human rights convention, government’s response to the refugee crisis, and the investigatory powers bill. It would be most beneficial if the Home Secretary was able to take her image into her own hands.
  3. At the same time, the Home Secretary is widely touted to be next in line for Leader of the Conservative Party, and has recently even hired ex-Sky News deputy political editor Joey Jones to be her new spokesperson and help manage her reputation in the leads up to it.
  4. The British public view the Home Secretary as a very serious and critical figure. Despite publicly announcing her support to remain in the EU, many of her past comments have reflected those of a Leave supporter. This means that for the Remain side, Theresa May is the best politician to influence the last phase of persuasion in the campaign. Having a powerful figure with the Home Secretary file who has a track record of being tough on crime and immigration will be a massive asset to persuade those teetering undecided in the referendum. The Home Secretary is the key to bringing the eurosceptics to pledge yes to Remain. In order for her to campaign effectively, she needs a good platform to do it on.
In turn, I would advise Home Secretary May to:
  1. Use Facebook as a platform to reach out to voters across the country who you’ve never been able to reach out to. We can build upon your existing voter contact base to identify additional similar voters by using our Custom Audiences and Lookalike Audiences tools.
  2. In the lead up to the SNP’s most successful election run in UK history, Nicola Sturgeon turned to our Q&A tool to activate voters from all parts of the country. The constant social interaction from the party was one of the large contributors to their success. While face-to-face outreach should never be neglected, consistent GOTV research shows that if people are more familiar with a name/figure, they are more likely to cast a ballot for them. For this, retargeting via Facebook ads and Q&A interactions will help.
  3. It is clear from past efforts that the Conservative demographic is both targetable and receptive on Facebook. Targeting digitally is more productive and precise way to reach voters. To both strengthen and build on supporters, use clear calls-to-action to obtain voter emails to activate support and collect pledges.
