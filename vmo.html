Serving as the Communications Manager for the Vancouver Mayor's Office

March 27, 2017 | Leah Bae
Tweet

Kevin Quinlan, Chief of Staff

Katie Robb, Director of Communications


People are drawn into public service because of an issue they care about. For me, that issue was education. Even as a student, I was driven to include more voices to create better civic systems. I built a not-for-profit advocacy organization (Student Voice Initiative) around this, working provincially with education policymakers to help institutionalize student voice practices across Canada. By harnessing the digital support of students across the country and employing a mix of traditional and digital media campaigns, I successfully lobbied for the establishment of a Student Trustee position on six school boards and influenced the establishment of many more. The civic leaders I worked with in the process were instrumental in developing my interest for strategic communications and outreach across local government.

Today, my passions and focus lie in the intersection of technology and politics, leveraging tech for public good. In the past four years in digital, I’ve advised and consulted for numerous politicians and community leaders on digital initiatives or youth engagement. I’ve spent six years volunteering and staffing on political and social campaigns on all levels of government in Canada, either in an outreach or digital capacity.

As someone who thrives off of human connection, I am well-networked and one of my personal mission statements is to build healthier societies by increasing civic and community engagement. I’m first and foremost a connecter: from mobilizing hundreds of volunteers for civic election campaigns to rallying ten thousands of TD employees around the new internal social networking platform, people are at the heart of everything I do. In my past experience as a digital consultant and trainer, I have educated thousands of businesspeople across Europe to adapt new digital practices and mindsets into their work, obtaining a certificate in personal coaching to support this. Whether I’m leading digital strategy workshops for Fortune 500 executives or helping politicians embed new process management software into their work, I love to help people understand the potential of technology.

I come from a digital-first background. I believe in harnessing digital to elevate human collaboration and empathy through the scale and convenience of technology. Working for a digital marketing agency, my work included writing content for KPMG’s digital entrepreneurship platform and designing influencer campaigns for a South Asian foods company. I optimized social ad campaigns to help clients develop ties with audiences we couldn’t organically reach.

Simultaneously, I understand the pressures, limitations, and opportunities of working for someone in public office. I’ve worked for Toronto Councillor Kristyn Wong-Tam, from auditing her online presence. to covering digital media for her events and constituent communications. I helped her world record-breaking event, Pride Wedding Toronto, trend first in Canadian Twitter and steal front pages of print/digital publications. I was also recruited by British Baroness Lane-Fox to work inside UK Parliament to advise former Labour cabinet minister Yvette Cooper MP in her digital strategy. Some of the actions I implemented included redesigning her website to invite more constituent engagement, utilizing social livestreaming for speeches, and deploying infographics to replace text-heavy announcements.

I use service-oriented agile methodologies to deliver my work. I am experienced in CMS management, digital asset design, HTML and CSS, on-page and off-page SEO, editing mobile-first visuals (e.g. videos, infographics, photos) executing social ads (e.g. Facebook, Google AdWords), writing (e.g. blogs, website copy, reports, traditional/social media), outreach to online influencers, and more. I’ve managed the social media channels of politicians and businesses alike, and was previously a digital marketing educator for large enterprises, primarily in the finance and media sectors. All in all, I am a very quick learner who is curious and coachable.

Housing, affordability, the environment, and the economy: they’re issues that cannot wait. I’m incredibly excited to leverage the speed and agility of digital to help accelerate the Mayor’s Office’s goals. I look very much forward to hearing from you and do let me know if there’s anything I can do to support you in the meantime.

Thank you,

Leah Bae
