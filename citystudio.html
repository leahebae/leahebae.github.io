    CityStudio: Five years later

    .

    CityStudio started in 2011 as the brainchild of two educators, Dr Janet Moore and Duane Elverum. What begun as a response to the City of Vancouver’s open ideas call for the Greenest City Action Plan, quickly caught the attention of students and city-builders alike.


    Fast forward 5+ years later, CityStudio has engaged over 3500 students, 113 faculty, and 60 of Vancouver staff, contributing 225 projects and over 125,000 hours of skills training and public sector innovation towards Vancouver’s Greenest City, Engaged City and Healthy City Strategies.


    It’s the product of continuous collaboration with the City of Vancouver, who had the willingness and open mind to work with undergraduate post-secondary students across the Lower Mainland to bring in new city-building initiatives. While our students may not have the direct experience necessary to implementing these ideas, the fresh perspective they bring has been touted as one of the most valuable assets in disrupting a traditional institution like City Hall. Our post-secondary partners at SFU, UBC, BCIT, ECUAD, Langara, and VCC have helped us create a space to activate the next generation of passionate city builders while giving back to the community today.


    As our students head into an increasingly uncertain future, the skills and real-life experiences they acquire at CityStudio helps prepare them for the future of work. These projects offer unconventional solutions to improve both our city and our students’ futures.
